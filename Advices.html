Stay at Home

The fewer people you are around, the lower the chance of infection. When you stay at home, you help stop the spread to others. But if you really need to go outside, Stay at least one-meter distance.

Keep a Safe Distance.

Maintain at least one-meter distance between yourself and others to reduce the risk of infection when they cough, sneeze or speak. Always remember that the further the distance, the better

Wash Your Hands Often.

Handwashing with soap removes the germs from hands. This helps prevent infection because: People frequently touch their eyes, nose and mouth without even realizinng it.

Cover your Cough.

It is not a good manner not to cover your mouth whenever you feel like coughing. Covering your mouth also reduces the spread or transmission of germs including the highly contagious influenza virus.

Sick? Consult ASAP.

Contacting your doctor soon after you experience symptoms is important especially during this quarantine. The possibility of transmission may occur which will result to further loses will happen if not handled responsibly.

Always Wear Mask

Nake wearing a mask a normal part of being around other people. The appropriate use, storage and cleaning or disposal are essential to make mask as effective as possible.

Avoid Closed Spaces.

Outbreaks have been reported in restaurants, choir practices, fitness classes, nightclubs offices and places places of worship where people have gathered, often in crowded indoor settings where they talk loudly, shout, breathe heavily or sing.

Avoid Crowded Places

The risk of getting COVID-19 are higher in crowded and inadequately ventilated spaces where people spend long periods of their time together in close proximity.

Avoid Touching your Eyes, Nose and Mouth

Hands touch many surfaces and can pick up viruses. Once contaminated, hands can transfer the your eyes, nose or mouth. From there the virus can enter your body and infect you.

Clean and Disinfect surfaces frequently.

Clean and Disinfect surfaces frequently especially those which are regularly touched, such as door handles, faucets and phone screens.

Stay Home and Self-Isolate when Feeling Unwell.

Stay home and self-isolate until you fully recover. Call your health care provider or hotline for advice. Have someone bring you supplies.

Open Window.

Increase the amount of natural ventilation when indoors. This helps avoid recirculated air. Recirculation mode should be avoided as much as possible.
